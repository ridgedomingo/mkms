1962

A group of nine practicing physicians residing in Makati, First conceived of the idea in 1962 of organizing a medical Society. The group felt the need for such an organization in the burgeoning and fast developing town.

1962

1962

After many sleepless nights and several bottles of Scotch and after talking to and convincing prospective members, they sought the assistance of the mother society the Rizal Medical Society, on the steps to be taken in order to qualify as a component member. Assisting them in the organizational mechanics was Dr. Guillermo Paez of the Malabon-Navotas Medical Society, also present during several informal caucus held were Dr. Ricardo T. Franco and Dr. Jose Quito Sr., also both from the Malabon-Navotal Medical Society, Dr. Alejandro Cruz Of the Pasig Medical Society and Dr. Ernesto P. Cruz of the Marikina Medical Society.

1962

1962

The first formal organizational meeting was held at Lizza’s Restaurant in Pasong Tamo, elected as Charter President was Dr. Alberto I. Estrella, son of the late Mayor of Makati, Mayor Maximo Estrella. The other Officers elected with him were as follows: Dr. Luis M. Rivera – Vice President; Dr. Aurora S. Genuino – Secretary; Dr. Romeo B. Adea – Asst. Secretary; Dr. Iva C. Anastacio – Treasurer; Dr. Godofredo Banzon – P.R.O. Councilors: Dr. Rodolfo L. Subida, Dr. Gregorio S. Borja, Jr., Dr. Gertrudo L. Mirasol and Dr. Claro Anastacio.

April 22, 1962

1962

The charter officers and members were inducted at the Capri International, Rufino Building, Ayala Avenue with then Secretary of Health Dr. Francisco Duque as Guest of Honor. Mayor Maximo Estrella inducted them into office. Also present during the occasion were Dr. Buenaventura Angtuaco, President of the Philippine Medical Association and officers and members of the Rizal Medical Society.

December 21, 1962

1962

Such was the short account of how the Makati Medical Society was born. The nine founding fathers felt that Makati was fast becoming a first class residential and business commercial center and they envisioned that a Medical Society has to be formed as quickly as possible for its members will be playing a vital role in the health care delivery System to an ever expanding and growing metropolis.

Twenty seven (27) years is a long time but it seems to be only yesterday. And yet considering the many projects, accomplishments and achievements of the Makati Medical Society for the welfare of the community and of its members, we who had a hand in the birth of the society, nurturing and caring for it during its early and “pledging” years, can now sit back with a little smile on our lips, a little misty eyed, unruffling our thinning and praying hair and whisper, “thank God… it was well worth it.”
